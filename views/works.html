Works

  • Creator Eileen Gray crafted a plush, comfortable seat known as the Bibendum chair in the 1900s and it is still widely recognized today as one of the 20th century’s most iconic furniture designs.
  • When Michelin launched an Italian travel magazine in 1907, Bibendum was given a regular column to pen his thoughts.
  • Bibendum got his first – and only – speaking engagement in December 1898 at a Paris cycle show: André Michelin had commissioned a large cardboard cutout of him to be set up at the Michelin booth, and instructed a cabaret comedian to crouch behind to provide animated banter.
